UK CCTV Privacy Laws 2026: What Homeowners Need to Know

Installing a CCTV system is one of the most effective ways to secure your property. However, as camera technology advances—with 4K resolution, audio recording, and AI-driven facial recognition becoming standard—the legal responsibilities of the homeowner have also increased.

If your domestic CCTV system captures images of people outside the boundary of your private domestic property—for example, in neighbours' homes or gardens, shared spaces, or on a public footpath or a street—then the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018 (DPA) will apply to you.

The 'Domestic Purposes' Exemption

Historically, homeowners relied on the "domestic purposes" exemption. If your cameras only captured your own property, data protection laws did not apply. This is still the case today. If your camera points strictly at your own driveway and garden, you are exempt from GDPR constraints.

However, the moment your camera's field of view (FOV) extends beyond your boundary, you become a "Data Controller" in the eyes of the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO).

Key Responsibilities if Recording Beyond Your Boundary

If your system records public spaces or a neighbour's property, you must comply with the following principles:

1. Clear Signage

You must inform people that recording is taking place. This is typically achieved by placing clear, visible signs at the boundary of your property. The sign should state that CCTV is in operation and, ideally, who to contact regarding the recordings.

2. Audio Recording Restrictions

Audio recording is highly intrusive. The ICO explicitly states that domestic CCTV should not record audio unless absolutely necessary. We strongly advise disabling the microphone on all exterior domestic cameras to avoid severe privacy breaches.

3. Subject Access Requests (SARs)

If you capture images of people outside your boundary, they have a legal right to request a copy of the footage in which they appear. You must provide this within one calendar month.

4. Data Retention

You should only keep the footage for as long as it is needed to protect your property. Most modern NVRs (Network Video Recorders) can be configured to automatically overwrite footage after 14 to 30 days. Keeping footage indefinitely is a breach of the DPA.

Privacy Masking: The Technical Solution

The easiest way to remain compliant without compromising security is to utilize "Privacy Masking." This is a software feature built into high-quality cameras (such as Hikvision or Dahua) that allows the installer to draw black, opaque boxes over specific areas of the camera's view.

By masking out the public pavement or the neighbour's window at the software level, the camera never records those areas, meaning you remain firmly within the domestic exemption.
